### Cheat Sheet for Solving Difficult Examples

#### Decision Procedures and Algorithms for Checking Implication

1. **General Approach**:
   - **Simplify Equations**: Break down complex nested expressions into simpler parts.
   - **Identify Patterns**: Look for common patterns or structures that imply certain properties (e.g., associativity, idempotence).
   - **Counterexamples**: Construct simple magma tables to find counterexamples where equations do not hold.

2. **Specific Techniques**:
   - **Equation Simplification**: Reduce nested expressions using associative and commutative properties.
   - **Variable Count and Operation Depth**: Analyze the number of variables and operations involved in each equation.
   - **Duality Analysis**: Check if equations are dual to each other (e.g., symmetric roles of elements).

#### Common Patterns Leading to TRUE or FALSE

- **TRUE Implications**:
  - Equations with nested structures that simplify to a single variable or constant.
  - Equations where operations commute and cancel out, leading to trivial solutions.

- **FALSE Implications**:
  - Equations involving non-commutative or non-associative operations.
  - Counterexamples showing contradictions in specific magma tables.

  - Counterexample: \( y \circ z = y \), \( w \circ u = w \), \( v = v \)
  - Result: \( x = y \)

#### Key Structural Properties

- **Variable Count**:
  - Equations with fewer variables often imply simpler structures.
  
- **Operation Depth**:
  - Nested operations can lead to complex implications. Simplify by reducing depth.

- **Duality**:
  - Check if equations are dual (e.g., \( x \circ y = z \) vs. \( y \circ x = z \)).
